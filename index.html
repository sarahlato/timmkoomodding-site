1: The Q3E 2nd Gen cannot run full Android, rather Raspbian has been ported to it.
2: The G3 is technically unsupported, however it has a similar enough OS to the G1 that modding may work. Try at your own risk. It is important to note that the G3 does allow app installation somewhat.
3: The Y1 1st Gen technically has issues with newer firmware versions and accurate trackpad mapping. This is being actively worked on.
4: The Smart Player has a different screen resolution but similar enough internals to the 2nd Gen that it may work with the same exploit. Try at your own risk.

Mod projects to use:

If you have the Q3 2nd Gen (curved back, Allwinner V3S chip), use jailbreak.mp3.
If you have the Q3 or Q5 3rd Gen (flat back, MT6582 chip, Holo design), back up your firmware using MTKClient before attempting to flash LithiumV1 using SP Flash Tool.
If you have the Q3 or Q5 4th Gen (flat back, smaller bezels, MT6582 chip, Holo design (Material Design in Poland, Germany before 2024)) or their Chinese counterpart, the Zhiyin Z1, use either Manufactory (the most stable and fastest generally, lacks some features, not actively maintained), or T-ROM (Based of the firmware manufactory is based on but with support for Google Play and Google login integration, slower).
If you have the Innioasis or TIMMKOO G1 (flat back, MT6753, Material 2), use Lumos G1.
If you have the Innioasis or TIMMKOO G3 (flat back, MT6753, Material 2), use Lumos G1 at your own risk.
If you have the Innioasis or TIMMKOO Y1 (touch wheel, MT6572, custom UI), use Rockbox-Y1
If you have the Q3/Q5 6th Gen (flat back, MT6582, Holo, no physical navbar), refer to Palutenfan123's resources.
